Goal

Intelligence Types Military INT Definitions applied to Bio-Emissions

SIGINT

SIGINT (COMINT & ELINT)

Intelligence derived from electronic signals and communications. Data through air-waves, electronic cabling, and space.

COMINT

COMINT

Intelligence derived from the communications between living entities.
Goal: Determine what is being said, to whom, and why.

Human Example: Voice, text, video between people
Animal Example: Bird Calls
Data Type: Audio files or Mel Spectrograms
ELINT

ELINT

Intelligence derived from non-communication signals from hardware and electronic signatures of equipment, electromagnetic (RF/radar) signals.
Goal: Determine capabilities, location, and operating frequencies of equipment.

Human Example: Equipment radar signatures
Animal Example: No animal equivalent because signals collected are electromagnetic; however bat echolocation is conceptually similar, but an acoustic instead of electromagnetic transmission.
Data Type: Inphase and Quadrature (I/Q) data and Pulse Descriptor Words (PDWs).
ACINT

ACINT

Intelligence derived from physical sounds unintentionally left behind by an object moving through the world.

Human Example: Sound of a jet engine
Animal Example: Sound of bird flapping its wings
Data Type: Audio files or Mel Spectrograms
MASINT

MASINT / Acoustic MASINT

MASINT: Analysis of unique physical signatures (both intentional active emissions, and unintentional physical or chemical byproducts) of an object.
Acoustic MASINT: Subset focusing on acoustic signatures (such as active sonar or vocalizations) transmitted to map or probe an environment.

Human Example: Submarine sonar
Animal Example: Bat echolocation
Data Type: Audio files or Mel Spectrograms
